THREE UNIQUE PLANTS IN NIT TRICHY

Kaaru Tulasi (Ocimum tenuiflorum) is an aromatic, erect, and well-branched herb. Commonly grown in East Asia, it reaches a height of around 30 - 100 cm. It has purple flowers. The leaves are green or purple, ovate, long, and slightly toothed.

Veldt Grape (Cissus quadrangularis): Veldt grape is a herb. It is a perennial plant of grapes. It is a freshly succulent creeper that is found all over India. It looks like a bunch of long fresh sticks. The stem is quadrangular with internodes every 4 to 15 cm depending on the length of the stem.

Holy basil (Ocimum gratissimum) : Its stem is erect around 1-2 m in height, glandular, odour aromatic. Leaves are elliptic to ovate, petiole around 6 cm long, odor aromatic, and taste characteristic.
